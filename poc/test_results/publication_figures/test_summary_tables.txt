Key Findings:
  • Architecture 1 achieves 13.8x better mean synchronization
  • Architecture 1 has 48.0x more stable timing (lower std dev)
  • Architecture 1 worst-case drift: 99 µs
  • Architecture 2 worst-case drift: 6449 µs (65.1x worse)

Conclusion:
  Architecture 1 (centralized UDP broadcast) demonstrates superior
  synchronization performance with sub-10µs mean drift, compared to
  Architecture 2's ~108µs mean drift. The direct triggering
  mechanism in Arch 1 eliminates clock drift accumulation that affects
  Arch 2's distributed execution model.
